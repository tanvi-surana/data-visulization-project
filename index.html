About this Visualization

This visualization uses the Interactive Slideshow to tell the story of WORLD. Built on scenes following a template of visual consistency, with various annotations, parameters and some triggers (will be explained more in the next two slides). More details of data cleaning information go here: ABOUT THE DATASET (slide 5).

The three scenes follow the structure as followings:

  • The first scene shows how the GDP impacts the number phones owned by people. With increase in GDP, the number of phones increases.
  • The first scene focuses on shows a comparison between literacy rates and infant mortality rates
  • The second scene focuses that literacy rate increases, the death rate decreases.

    • The three charts in this visualization:

  • Bar charts have been used to compare the literacy rates and how they impact infant mortality rates
  • The scatterplot here is showing that countries where literacy is high have low death rates
  • The scatterplot here explains how with increase in GDP of a nation, the people owning phones increase

    Elements of This Visualization
    1. Scenes

      2. There are 16 slides, including four scenes with single chart of each scene in this presentation. Each slide can be navigated through guiding arrows in the right bottom corner. Press Esc key at any point to get an overview of all slides.

    2. Parameters

      3. The main parameters of this story are Regions & Country names, corresponding value of Phones, GDP, infant mortality rates, service, industry, agriculture and Literacy.

    3. Triggers

      4. Legends in the scatter plot help us get data in the plot for that particular region of the world. The scatter plot has two buttons. One can be used to zoom into the graph and the other can be used to interchange the axes. The bar plots also have legends that help us understand the graph better

    Elements of This Visualization (continued)

    4. Annotations: including tooltips, legends and labels; follow a template of visual consistency.

    Trends of data in both line chart and bar chart show up by clicking the "down" arrow within the slide.
    Tooltips follow a template for visual consistency (see file "tooltip.js" for details):
    1. Tooltips in Gross Domestic Product and Phones scatter plot show the GDP value, number of phones value and the region name show up by moving mouse over a the plot. Tooltip disappears by moving mouse off a certain bubble.
    2. Tooltips in bar chart shows up by moving mouse over a bar. When a tooltip shows up, the corresponding bar will be highlighted by changing the filled color and changing stroke color to black. Tooltip disappears by moving mouse off a certain bar. Labels:
    1. Labels in area donut chart shown in the center of each bubble with information of area name and corresponding average life expectancy.
    2. Labels are present in both scatterplot and bar chart
    Legends:
    1. Legends for country in bar plots.
    2. Legends in bar chart: assign original filled colors to Infant Mortality and Literacy. 3. Legends have been used in donut charts for labelling service, agriculture and industry.
